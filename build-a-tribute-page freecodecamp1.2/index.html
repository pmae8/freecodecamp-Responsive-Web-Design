Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug, an American agronomist, is known as the "Father of the Green Revolution" for his work in developing high-yielding, disease-resistant wheat varieties. His research and innovations significantly increased agricultural production worldwide, particularly in developing countries, helping to avert famines and save millions of lives.

He was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970 for his contributions to world peace through increasing food supply. Borlaug's work continues to inspire scientists and agriculturalists in the fight against hunger and poverty.
